# Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 1, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Razorglint Labs / TCOG Collective LLC handles information related to ComplianceDriftDetector products, subscriptions, assisted reviews, and support.

## 1. Data We Collect

We may receive:

- Contact information provided through Stripe checkout, such as name, email address, business name, and billing details
- Transaction information from Stripe
- Support messages sent by the customer
- Files or text the customer voluntarily provides for assisted report review

We do not require production credentials, API keys, secrets, customer PII, source code, or full database dumps.

## 2. Payment Processing

Payments are processed by Stripe. We do not store card numbers or payment credentials. Stripe processes payment information under its own terms and privacy policy.

## 3. Customer Input Data

For self-service local use, customer input data stays on the customer's machine.

For assisted reviews, customers should provide only anonymized, redacted policy claims and behavior evidence. Customers are responsible for removing secrets, credentials, personal data, and confidential third-party information before sending materials.

## 4. How We Use Data

We use customer information to:

- Process subscriptions and payments
- Provide access, updates, and support
- Perform assisted report reviews when requested
- Communicate about product updates, support, billing, and service issues

We do not sell customer data.

## 5. Data Retention

Support and assisted-review materials may be retained only as long as reasonably needed to provide the service, resolve disputes, maintain records, or comply with legal obligations.

Customers may request deletion of assisted-review materials by contacting support.

## 6. Third Parties

Stripe is used for payment processing. GitHub may host public release artifacts and documentation. Email providers may process support communications.

## 7. Security

We use reasonable care to protect customer communications and assisted-review materials, but no method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure.

Do not send secrets, credentials, production tokens, customer PII, or sensitive live-system data.
