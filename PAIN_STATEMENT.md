# Pain Statement

## The 30-Second Version

**Who feels this:** Compliance officers, engineering VPs, CISOs at companies with written policies that are supposed to govern system behavior — but nobody continuously checks if reality still matches policy.

**The pain:** Your policy says one thing. Your system does another. The gap grows silently for months. You discover it during an audit, a breach, or a lawsuit — never proactively. By then, you can't prove when drift started or how long you were non-compliant.

**What this gives you:** A continuous drift measurement between policy declarations and system behavior. Every policy claim is scored against real evidence. Drift trends are visible over time. The moment behavior diverges from policy, it's flagged — with a hash-anchored timestamp proving when the gap opened.

**The artifact:** A downloadable drift report that shows: which policies are being followed, which are drifting, which are violated, and which system behaviors have no policy at all. Hash-sealed for tamper evidence.

## Why This Matters Now

- EU AI Act Article 9 requires risk management systems to be maintained — not just established
- ISO 27001 auditors test for "continual improvement" — which means you need evidence of continuous alignment
- SOC 2 Type II covers a period, not a moment — drift during the period is a finding
- Insurance renewals increasingly require proof of governance consistency
- Regulators are shifting from "do you have a policy?" to "does your system follow it?"
